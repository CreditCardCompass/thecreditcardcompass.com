Capital One Venture X vs Chase Sapphire Reserve: Which Wins in 2026?

⏱ 9 min read✍️ The Credit Card Compass🔄 Last updated April 2026
Quick answer: The Capital One Venture X wins on value — after credits and anniversary miles, the effective annual fee is nearly $0. The Chase Sapphire Reserve wins on rewards for dining and more flexible transfer partners. For most travelers, the Venture X is the smarter financial choice. For foodies and Chase ecosystem users, the Reserve makes more sense.

The Big Picture

When Capital One launched the Venture X in 2021, it was a direct shot at the Chase Sapphire Reserve. Same travel credit ($300 each). Same lounge access (Priority Pass). Premium travel protections. But $155 cheaper per year.

Four years later, the Venture X has proven itself as a legitimate premium card — and for many travelers, it's actually the better choice. But the Sapphire Reserve has real advantages in rewards rates and transfer partners that keep it relevant.

Here's the honest breakdown of which card wins where.

Annual Fees and Credits

Venture X — $395/year

The $300 travel credit applies to bookings through Capital One Travel — flights, hotels, and rental cars. Combined with the 10,000 anniversary miles (worth at least $100), the math is remarkable: $395 fee minus $300 credit minus $100 in anniversary miles = effective annual cost of -$5. You're essentially paid to hold this card.

Sapphire Reserve — $550/year

The $300 travel credit applies automatically to any travel purchase — no portal required. Uber, taxis, parking, airlines, hotels — it all counts. After the credit the effective fee is $250. That's still $255 more than the Venture X's effective cost.

Winner — Annual Fee Value
Capital One Venture X
Effective fee of -$5 vs $250 for the Reserve. Not even close on pure fee math.

Rewards and Earning Rates

Venture X

Earns 10x on hotels and rental cars booked through Capital One Travel, 5x on flights through Capital One Travel, and a flat 2x on every other purchase. That 2x flat rate is exceptional — no premium card matches it for everyday spending outside of travel portals.

Sapphire Reserve

Earns 3x on all travel and dining — two of the most common premium cardholder spending categories. The dining rate is the key differentiator. If you spend $800/month on dining, the Reserve earns 2,400 more points per month than the Venture X on that category alone.

Points are worth 1.5¢ each through Chase Travel, vs 1¢ for Venture X miles in most redemptions.

Winner — Dining & Everyday Rewards
Chase Sapphire Reserve
3x on dining beats 2x for heavy restaurant spenders. 1.5¢ point value is 50% more than Venture X's standard rate.

Lounge Access

Both cards include Priority Pass Select membership — access to 1,300+ airport lounges worldwide. The key difference is the proprietary lounge networks.

The Venture X adds access to Capital One Lounges — currently at Dallas, Denver, Las Vegas, and Washington Dulles, with more opening. These lounges are spacious, well-designed, and consistently rated excellent by travelers who've visited them.

The Sapphire Reserve adds access to Chase Sapphire Lounges — currently in Boston, Hong Kong, Las Vegas, New York JFK, and a few other locations. Also well-reviewed.

Winner — Lounge Access
Tie
Both have Priority Pass plus their own growing lounge networks. The better choice depends on which airports you fly through most often.

Transfer Partners

Chase Ultimate Rewards transfers to 14 partners including United, Southwest, Hyatt, Marriott, British Airways, Air France/KLM, and Singapore Airlines. The Hyatt partnership is particularly valuable — Hyatt points can be worth 2¢+ at luxury properties.

Capital One Miles transfer to 15+ partners including Air Canada, Turkish Airlines, Avianca, Singapore Airlines, and Wyndham. No major US airline is a direct partner — the closest is Air Canada (which connects to Star Alliance, including United flights).

Winner — Transfer Partners
Chase Sapphire Reserve
United and Southwest are direct partners — two of the most useful US airlines. Hyatt transfers are among the most valuable in points.

So Who Should Get Which Card?

Get the Capital One Venture X if:

  • You want maximum value at the lowest effective cost
  • You spend heavily on everyday purchases (2x flat rate wins here)
  • You're flexible on airlines and don't need US airline-specific transfers
  • You book hotels and cars through travel portals (10x is exceptional)
  • You don't have strong loyalty to Chase's partner ecosystem

Get the Chase Sapphire Reserve if:

  • You spend a lot on dining and want 3x rewards
  • You fly United or Southwest regularly and want transfer access
  • You stay at Hyatt hotels and value those transfer redemptions
  • You already use other Chase cards (Freedom Unlimited, Ink) and want to pool points
  • You want the automatic $300 travel credit with no portal requirement

Our honest pick: For most travelers, the Capital One Venture X is the better deal. The effective annual fee math is hard to argue with. But if dining rewards and Chase's transfer ecosystem matter to you — especially United, Southwest, and Hyatt — the Sapphire Reserve delivers more targeted value.
