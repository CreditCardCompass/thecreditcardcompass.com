Best Credit Cards for Delta Flyers in 2026

⏱ 8 min read✍️ The Credit Card Compass🔄 Last updated April 2026
Quick answer: The Delta SkyMiles Reserve Amex is the best card for dedicated Delta loyalists who want Sky Club access and a companion certificate. The Amex Platinum is the better choice for Delta flyers who also travel on other airlines. The Amex Gold is the best value option if you're a moderate Delta flyer who spends heavily on dining.

Why Your Credit Card Matters for Delta Flyers

Delta Air Lines has one of the most credit card-friendly ecosystems in aviation — and American Express is the exclusive credit card partner. That means if you want to earn SkyMiles through a credit card, transfer points to Delta, or access Delta Sky Club lounges through a card benefit, you're in the Amex world.

This exclusivity is actually good for Delta loyalists. It keeps the SkyMiles program valuable and means Amex cards have genuinely strong Delta-specific perks. But it also means your choice of card matters — the wrong Amex card for your travel style leaves real money on the table.

Key fact: Only American Express cards transfer points to Delta SkyMiles. Chase, Capital One, and Citi do not have Delta as a transfer partner. If Delta is your primary airline, your card strategy starts with Amex.

The Best Two-Card Strategy for Delta Flyers

Many serious Delta flyers carry two cards — the Delta SkyMiles Reserve for Delta-specific perks and Sky Club access, paired with the Amex Gold for everyday dining and grocery spending. This gives you:

  • Sky Club access on Delta flights
  • First checked bag free for companions
  • Companion certificate annually
  • 4x on dining and groceries building SkyMiles through transfers
  • Effective annual cost: ~$900 minus companion certificate value
